Convolution Visualizer

Slide one signal across another, inspect the overlap product, and watch the convolution integral build point by point.

ECE 205 / 2A

Multiply the overlap, then accumulate the area.

Use presets or enter your own expressions for f(t) and g(t). The visualizer flips and shifts g, shades the pointwise product, and traces the final convolution output at the selected time.

Convolution Model

y(t) = integral f(tau) g(t - tau) d tau

Domain: -6 to 6 with numerical integration over 600 samples.

Stage 01

Input Signals

Sample f(t) and g(t) across the same time window before the convolution sweep begins.

Stage 02

Flip, Shift, and Multiply

Drag across the chart or use the scrubber to position t. The gold fill shows the current product f(tau) g(t - tau).

The vertical marker sets the output time. When the overlap is large, the convolution value at that time grows.

Stage 03

Convolution Output

The faint path shows the full numerical convolution, while the highlighted trace fills in up to the current t.
