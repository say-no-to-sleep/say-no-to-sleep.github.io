Computer Engineering Tutor

Informal tutoring for Grade 10-12 Computer Engineering students across circuit building, programming, Raspberry Pi, PLCs, and independent projects.

Supporting Student Projects

During Grade 12 spare periods, I helped students in Computer Engineering classes troubleshoot and develop projects. My approach was to understand what they were trying to build, then point them toward a fix rather than directly giving them the answer.

  • Helped Grade 10 students with Arduino circuits, LED marquee projects, and common wiring issues.
  • Supported Grade 12 Raspberry Pi work, especially Linux terminal usage and component integration.
  • Helped students brainstorm and refine self-proposed project ideas.

Tutorials and Lessons

I created and shared several resources for topics students were struggling with, including IR remote implementation, I2C display communication, and Siemens PLC ladder programming.

I also introduced hardware topics such as the L293D motor driver and Arduino pin layouts, often through hands-on project examples.

Takeaways

  • Improved my ability to explain technical concepts to students with different levels of experience.
  • Got faster at diagnosing circuit and code problems from limited context.
  • Reinforced my own technical knowledge by teaching it to others.
  • Confirmed that I enjoy teaching and technical mentorship.
